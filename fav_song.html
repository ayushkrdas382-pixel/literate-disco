MY FAVOURITE SONGS

Music has always been a vast landscape for me, a journey spanning the grandiosity of classical compositions to the infectious energy of modern pop. Yet, among the thousands of melodies I’ve heard, only a select few truly pierce the silence of my soul. There is a specific, haunting beauty in the way songs like Kaifi Khalil’s Kahani Suno 2.0 or Anuv Jain’s Husn capture the ache of a breaking heart, echoing the timeless, soulful depth of Kailash Kher’s Teri Deewani. While I find myself mesmerized by the modern mastery of Arijit Singh, I am always pulled back to the nostalgic magic of Kishore Kumar; specifically, the breezy, moonlit romance of Yeh Raatein Yeh Mausam remains the ultimate anchor of my musical heart.

This same emotional thread weaves through my love for English music, where I am drawn to melodies that feel like a raw, honest confession. Whether it is the desperate crescendo of Tom Odell’s Another Love, the cinematic weight of Adele’s Skyfall, or the delicate vulnerability of Ruth B’s Dandelions, I find myself seeking sounds that mirror my own inner world. Every artist, from the rhythmic soul of Libianca to the quirky melancholy of Ricky Montgomery, plays a part in my story, but it is Gigi Perez’s Sailor Song that currently holds my heart closest, serving as a gentle, melodic reminder of the feelings words often fail to express.
